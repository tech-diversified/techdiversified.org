Frequently Asked Questions

Why did you start TechDiversified?
In 2014, some of the world's leading technology companies released their diversity metrics, confirming what many already knew -
people of color and women are largely excluded from high-paying tech jobs.
It is time to level the playing field.
We want to help develop and funnel talented people of color into high-paying tech jobs. This will help companies find qualified individuals for their unfilled positions as well as provide higher income salaries for populations that typically earn less than average.
Is Tech Diversified open to everyone?
Yes. Inclusivity is very important to us. Although our target audience is people of color, our primary goal is to have a positive learning environment that welcomes everyone regardless of race, age, religion, sexual orientation, disability or education - we want to work with everyone.
What is the best way to contact Tech Diversified?
By e-mail. info@techdiversified.org
Do I need any experience to participate in the technical workshops?
The technical workshops may be most valuable to those who have some familiarity with technology or programming concepts, but ALL are welcome to attend.
Where are workshops & meetups held?
Our space is graciously provided by SpatialDev and is located in Seattle’s historic and trendy Ballard neighborhood (just north of downtown Seattle).
Many workshops will happen in the Ballard office, although some workshops may take place in local coffee shops or other meeting areas, depending on convenience and availability. Check out the Tech Diversified events page or the Meetup Page for details on the location of specific workshops.
Are you a 501 (c) (3)?
We have submitted our application for 501 (c ) (3) status in 2014. We will update our status as soon as our status has been accepted.
What are the dates of the workshops?
Workshop dates will be announced via the events page or the Meetup Page.
Do I have to pay to participate in Tech Diversified?
No. Tech Diversified is free for members because of the hard work of volunteers and generous donations.
What if I don't have my own computer?
While we would love to supply everyone with a computer – we have not reached that point yet. Members are expected to bring their own machines, though we may be able to supply a limited number of loaners if necessary.
